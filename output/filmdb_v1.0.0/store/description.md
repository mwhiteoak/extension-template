# FilmDB — Film Stock Companion for B&H, Adorama & eBay

Stop guessing which film to buy. FilmDB overlays grain, exposure latitude, colour profile, and community sample scans directly onto product pages at the retailers you already shop.

## What you see at a glance

While browsing B&H Photo, Adorama, Film Photography Store, or eBay, FilmDB pops an overlay panel the moment it recognises a film stock:

- **Grain** — fine / medium / coarse
- **Latitude** — stops over- and under-exposure the film can handle
- **Colour profile** — warm / neutral / cool / high-contrast / B&W
- **Process** — C-41, B&W, or E-6 (slide)
- **Sample scans** from Analog.cafe — real photos shot on that stock, shown as thumbnails you can click through

## 27 films, 8 brands — all major stocks covered

Kodak · Ilford · Fujifilm · CineStill · Lomography · Rollei · Foma · Kosmo Foto

Colour negative, black-and-white, and slide stocks — if your local shop carries it, FilmDB almost certainly knows it.

## Expired film on eBay? Know what it's worth.

Searching eBay for a roll of expired Portra or expired Velvia? FilmDB detects expired-film listings and pulls live sold-price benchmarks (last 90 days) from the eBay marketplace — median price per roll, sample count, and a Good/Fair/Pricey badge relative to the current listing.

> Requires a free eBay Developer App ID configured in the extension options.

## Lightweight and unobtrusive

- Overlay appears in a collapsible side panel — dismiss it once per page and it stays out of the way
- No account required
- No data leaves your browser (film database is bundled; eBay calls are made directly with your own App ID)
- Manifest V3
